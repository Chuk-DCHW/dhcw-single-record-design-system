# DHCW User-Centred Design Principles

These 9 principles guide how we design with people in mind. They are approved by the Technical Design Authority (TDA) and standardise how DHCW designs and delivers inclusive, user-centred, and outcome-driven services.

All design and engineering work in this design system must align with these principles.

---

## 1. Start with user needs

- Test your assumptions.
- Public services are for everyone and must be driven by user needs.
- User research prevents wasted resources and reveals real problems, enabling evidence-based, cost-effective decisions.

---

## 2. Design with data

- Use data to make decisions.
- Continuously monitor service performance and use data and user feedback to prioritise improvements and identify problems that need to be fixed.

---

## 3. Understand context

- Understand users and their needs in the context of health and care.

---

## 4. Design services in Welsh and English

- Design and build services that promote and ease the use of Welsh.
- Treat those who speak Welsh equally with those who speak English.

---

## 5. Make it simple and inclusive

- Make the service easy to use and make sure everyone can use it.
- Design inclusively, ensuring easy access for all, including those often excluded.
- Healthcare can be complex — do the hard work to make it simpler.

---

## 6. Design for trust and safety

- Digital information, tools, and services have the potential to cause patient harm.
- Services must be secure to maintain users' trust.
- Respect and protect users' confidentiality and privacy.

---

## 7. Deliver with transparency and consistency

- Use and contribute to open standards, common components, and patterns.
- Share what you are doing whenever you can.

---

## 8. Iterate, learn and improve

- Rapidly iterate and focus on the improvements that have the most value, based on user feedback.
- Use an incremental, fast-paced approach to get working products into users' hands as early as possible, as often as possible.

---

## 9. Minimise environmental impact

- The climate crisis is a health crisis.
- Follow sustainability best practice to reduce the environmental impact of your service across its lifespan.

---

## Source and governance

These principles are maintained by DHCW and approved by the Technical Design Authority. They align with the [GDS Service Standard](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/service-standard) and [NHS England design principles](https://service-manual.nhs.uk/design-system).

Any proposed changes to these principles must be approved by the TDA and recorded as a DDR in `/decisions/`.
